F.A.Q

Frequently Asked Questions

  • How does the road tolling system work?

    The road tolling system employs RFID technology to collect tolls from vehicles passing through designated toll booths. It aims to streamline the toll collection process and enhance overall traffic management.

  • What types of vehicles are subject to tolls?

    Tolls are applicable to various types of vehicles, including cars, trucks, and motorcycles. Each vehicle type may have a different toll rate based on factors such as size and weight.

  • How are toll rates determined?

    Toll rates are determined based on various factors, including the type of vehicle, distance traveled, and any additional services provided. The goal is to establish a fair and sustainable pricing structure.

  • How can I pay tolls?

    Tolls can be paid through various methods, including electronic toll collection (ETC) systems, credit/debit cards and, in some cases, traditional cash payments at toll booths.

  • Is my personal information safe when using electronic toll collection?

    Yes, electronic toll collection systems prioritize the security and privacy of user data. Advanced encryption methods and security protocols are in place to protect personal information.

  • Are toll rates subject to change?

    Toll rates may be subject to periodic reviews and adjustments based on factors such as inflation, maintenance costs, and infrastructure improvements. Users will be notified in advance of any changes.
